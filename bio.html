About Me

I grew up in Camden, Maine in a half Russian, half American household.
I was always interested in biology and history, and planned to become a doctor, like my eldest brother.
However, upon arriving at Tufts, I almost immediately found a new calling in computer science, and after
attending one intro biology lecture and one intro computer science lecture, I dropped bio and haven't looked back
I haven't lost my love of history, hence my history computer science double majors; neither have I lost a love for the
outdoors that I found growing up in rural Maine. I joined the Tufts Mountain Club as soon as I arrived at Tufts
and was elected to my first of two terms on the TMC executive board during my first semester at Tufts.
I also gained a love of public speaking during my employment as a lecturer at Hope Elephants. In order to continue
with that passion I joined the TEDxTufts group as a speaker coach during my first semester at school. I firmly believe
it's of the utmost importance that people have an understanding of both quantitative and qualitative disciplines, and
I hope my choice of clubs and classes at Tufts illustrates that.
